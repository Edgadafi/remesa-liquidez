## Memory test (repeat after one hearing)

> **"Mandas $100 a México. TIA elige si tu familia cobra en el cajero más cercano o en la tiendita del barrio — y le avisa por WhatsApp. Pagas 25 centavos, no siete dólares."**

---

## Layer 1 — PROBLEM

**What's broken?**

Sending $100 US→MX costs **~$7** and takes **days**. The receiver often has **no bank** — only WhatsApp. Even when money "arrives," there's **no physical liquidity** where they live: the corner store can't cover the amount, or there's no ATM nearby.

**Specific line:**
> *"Your family pays $7 per $100, waits days, and still doesn't know where to pick up cash."*

---

## Layer 2 — INSIGHT

**What do you see that nobody else does?**

The bottleneck isn't moving dollars across borders — it's the **last meter**: cash in hand, with trust, without an app.

- **Urban:** needs density → ATMs when tienditas are dry
- **Rural:** needs trust → tienditas that recycle local cash float

**Nobody routes between both.** Remittance apps optimize senders. Banks optimize branches. **TIA optimizes physical liquidity.**

---

## Layer 3 — MECHANISM

**How does it work?**

1. Sender locks USDC in Solana escrow
2. **TIA routing agent** picks best cash-out rail:
   - **Urban / low tiendita liquidity** → allied ATM (cardless withdrawal)
   - **Rural / isolated** → local tiendita (merchant float)
3. Receiver gets **WhatsApp** with exact instructions
4. Cash in hand · 0.25% fee on-chain

**Specific line:**
> *"Blockchain holds the money. TIA picks where you pick up the cash. WhatsApp tells you how."*
