## The problem (sender-first)

Senders to Mexico **can't automate instant, recurring transfers** because TradFi stacks friction on top of friction:

| TradFi pain | What it costs senders |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Hidden fees (5–8%) | Up to **7% of capital** lost per transfer |
| Slow settlement (1–3 days) | **Hours of waiting** + anxiety per txn |
| No programmable recurrence | Manual trip to Western Union every payday |
| Opaque FX spreads | Can't verify what family actually receives |
| Receiver locked out | Family needs bank branch or app they don't have |

**Staying stuck isn't neutral.** Every month, diaspora families bleed margin and time on the same broken loop.

---

## The cost of inaction

```
$500/month sent × 7% fee     = $420/year lost to intermediaries
× 12 manual runs/year        = 12+ hours in lines, forms, phone calls
× zero automation              = missed paydays when life gets busy
```

**Time to build better.**

---

## What @remesatia is building

**Remesa TIA** — an agentic cross-border liquidity layer for US→MX:

1. **Send** — USDC on Solana; fee **0.25%** on-chain, not 7%
2. **Automate** *(roadmap)* — recurring reservations; set once, TIA runs every payday
3. **Settle** — ~400ms on-chain vs days in TradFi
4. **Route** — TIA picks best cash-out: **ATM** (urban) or **tiendita** (rural)
5. **Notify** — WhatsApp to family: where, when, how much — no app install

> Today (Bridge MVP): instant single transfer + hybrid routing vision + tiendita rail live on devnet.  
> Next (Q3 pilot): recurring keeper + ATM partner.
