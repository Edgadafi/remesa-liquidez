## Slide 11
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
1 — COVER
Name, logo, headline.


Why: it's the only sentence guaranteed to be read.

Trap: buzzword soup. "AI-powered RWA infrastructure" = closed tab.
11	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 12
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
2 — PROBLEM
Problem statement.


Why: pain is the purchase driver.

Trap: three problems = no problem.
12	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 13
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
3 — PROOF OF THE PROBLEM
2-3 pieces of external evidence: newspaper headlines, survey
stats, a one-line customer story.


Why: claims are yours; evidence is the world's.

Trap: stats without sources.
13	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 14
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
4 — SOLUTION
Value proposition with mechanism (one line of what it is, one
visual)


Why: outcome first so they care, mechanism so they believe.

Trap: the feature dump.
14	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 15
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
5 — OPPORTUNITY
Market size with a source, and your beachhead: the first segment
you'll win.


Why: big number = worth the fund's time; beachhead = you know
where to start.

Trap: "we'll capture 1% of a trillion".
15	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 16
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
6 — TRACTION
2-3 numbers with dates: TVL, users, LOIs, pilots, growth rate.


Why: traction is the only slide investors can't argue with.

Trap: vanity metrics (Discord members, impressions).
16	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 17
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
7 — PARTNERS
Logos grouped by function, labelled honestly: live / signed / in
discussion.


Why: borrowed credibility is still credibility, and in crypto,
distribution.

Trap: logo inflation. One diligence call exposes it and kills all trust.
17	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 18
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
8 — Business model
Who pays, how much, and where value accrues: equity, token, or
both.


Why: the most fumbled question in every onchain Q&A.

Trap: "the token captures value" with no mechanism.
18	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 19
THE 10-SLIDE BLUEPRINT
9 — TEAM
Faces, recognisable logos, one proof line each, how long you've
worked together.


Why: at pre-seed the team is the product.

Trap: listing hobbies instead of proof. Investors check LinkedIn
within minutes.
19	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

10 — ASK

direct contact.


Why: it's read first. It must stand alone.

Trap: vague use of funds ("engineering and growth").

## Slide 44
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
1 — HEADER
Logo, 2-3 links max (Docs, X), one CTA button.


Why: the header is navigation for the convinced, not a sitemap.

Trap: seven links = seven escape hatches.
44	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 45
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
2 — HERO
Headline, subheadline, direct CTA.


Why: this is where the Grunt test is passed or failed.

Trap: clever over clear.
45	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 46
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
3 — SOCIAL PROOF
Names and numbers. Backers, audits, integrations, live metrics (TVL,
users, volume).


Why: credibility borrowed is credibility earned, if it's honest.

Trap: logo inflation. One click on a block explorer exposes it.
46	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 47
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
4 — PROBLEM
Problem statement.


Why: the customer/user is the hero. You are the guide.

Trap: opening with your story ("we are a next-generation...").
47	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 48
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
5 — SOLUTION
Value proposition.


Why: the problem created the want. Now name the fix, before
explaining it.

Trap: explaining how it works before saying what it is.
48	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 49
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
6 — BENEFITS
Three benefits, phrased as outcomes for the user.


Why: people buy outcomes, not features.

Trap: the feature dump. Thirteen bullets, nobody remembers three.
49	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 50
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
7 — HOW IT WORKS
Three named steps. Connect → Deposit → Earn. Or: Book a call →
Get your plan → Ship.


Why: people don't act when acting feels foggy. A plan makes the
step feel small and safe.

Trap: the architecture diagram nobody asked for.
50	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 51
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
8 — POSITIONING
You versus the alternatives already in the visitor's head. 2-3 contrasts.
Don’t name competition.


Why: different only lands against a reference point.

Trap: "we're unique" with no comparison. Unique compared to what?
51	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 52
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
9 — BELIEFS
3-4 convictions, stated flat. No VC. Open source. Immutable contracts.


Why: it tells your reader "you are one of us". Identity converts harder
than features.

Trap: beliefs you can't be held to. Immutable is a promise with a block
explorer receipt.
52	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 53
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
10 — FINAL CT	A
The same one action, restated. "Launch app" "Book a call"


Why: conviction peaks at the bottom of the page. Ask again.

Trap: introducing a new, second action here.
53	© 2026 Kerna. All rights reserved.

## Slide 54
THE 10-SECTION BLUEPRINT
11 — FOOTER
Everything else. X, GitHub, Docs, audits, media kit, manifesto,
contact, legal.


Why: the footer serves your secondary readers: investors, press,
hires, integrators.

Trap: burying body-level trust assets. Audits deserve daylight.
